Mapo Tofu

Recipe Source: New York Times Recipes

Description

Mapo Tofu is a traditional chinese dish from the Sichuan Region. It consists of tofu set in a spicy sauce, typically a thin, oily, and bright red suspension, based on douban, and douchi, along with minced meat, traditionally beef.

Ingredients

  • 16 oz - Soft Tofu
  • 1 tsp - Sichuan Peppercorns
  • 3 tbsp - canola oil
  • 6 oz - ground beef or pork
  • 2.5-3 tbsp - doubanjiang
  • 1 tsp - douchi
  • 1 tsp - minced fresh ginger
  • 0.5 tsp red-pepper flakes(optional)
  • 1 tsp - soy sauce
  • 1 tsp - granulated sugar
  • Fine sea salt
  • 2 scallions, trimmed and cut
  • 1.5 tbsp - cornstarch in 3 tbsp water
  • Cooked White Rice

Procedure

  1. Cut tofu into 3/4 in cubes and put into bowl. Pour boiling water over tou to cover. Let sit for 15 mins
  2. In wok over medium heat, toast peppercorns for 2-3 mins until fragrant, darkened, slight smoke. :et cool, pound until grainy.
  3. Drain tofu reserving 1.5 cups of soaking water.
  4. Reheat wok over high. When hot, swirl oil in and add meat. Mash meat into oil until cooked and crumbly(1-2 mins).
  5. Add 2.5 tbsp doubanjian, douchi, giner, red-pepper flakes. Cook 2 mins, stirring constantly until reddish brown. Add soy sauce and sugar, stir to combine, then add tofu. Combine ingredients without breaking up tofu much.
  6. Add soaking water, bring to summer, cook for 3 mins, to let tofu absorb sauce.
  7. Slightly lower heat and taste sauce. Add reaming .5 tbsp doubanjian for heat if needed, pinch of salt, or sprinkly of sugar.
  8. Add scallions and stir to combine. Stir in cornstach slurry, enough for map to thicken. Sprinkle in peppercorns, given the last stir. Serve on hot rice.
